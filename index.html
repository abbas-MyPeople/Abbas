What's possible right now

Real things restaurants are doing with AI today.

A snapshot of what's working out there — with an honest note on when each is a fit, and when it isn't. Knowing the difference is the whole point.

AI that answers every call

A voice agent takes orders and books tables 24/7, so no rush ever sends money to voicemail.

Fit if you lose takeout/reservation calls during peaks.

Maybe not if your call volume is genuinely low.

AI drive-thru & ordering upsell

Order-taking AI that never forgets to upsell, lifting average ticket on every order.

Fit if you're high-volume QSR / multi-unit.

Maybe not for a single low-volume or full-service spot.

Forecasting that schedules itself

Predict demand by the hour from your own sales, weather, and events — then staff and prep to match.

Fit if labor is your biggest controllable cost.

Maybe not if covers are already known from the book.

Reviews that grow themselves

AI invites happy guests to review and drafts on-brand replies — and a higher rating moves real revenue.

Fit if you're an independent (a 1★ lift ≈ 5–9% revenue).

Maybe not as a priority for big chains.

Vision AI that kills food waste

A camera-and-scale watches the bin and tells you exactly what you're throwing away, and why.

Fit if you run buffets, banquets, or heavy prep.

Maybe not for a small, tight-portion kitchen.

Loyalty that knows each guest

AI personalizes offers to each diner's habits to drive real repeat visits — not blanket discounts.

Fit if you have the volume and data (often multi-unit).

Maybe not for low-frequency fine dining.
