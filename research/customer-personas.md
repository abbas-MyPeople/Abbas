# Customer Personas — AZ Restaurant Partners (small US restaurants only)

**Evidence tags:** **[IND]** independent data/real operator voice · **[SOFT]** blog · **[VENDOR]** company (buyer-behavior signal, biased) · **(INFERENCE)** reasoning beyond evidence.

**Macro baseline framing all three:** only **42% of US restaurants were profitable in 2024**; independent net margins **3–5%**, prime cost **55–65%**, food **35%+ above pre-pandemic**; **96% cite rising labor / 95% rising food costs**; **83% say tech gives advantage but only 28% say it improved profitability** (the expectation/outcome gap that defines small-operator skepticism); James Beard/Deloitte 2025: **>70% saw fewer patrons**, **72% raised wages >10%**. [IND, NRA SOI 2025 / JBF-Deloitte] → *Squeezed, told "tech helps," largely burned or overwhelmed. That tension is the wedge.*

---

## P1 — "Maria & Sam": mom-and-pop single location (family/immigrant-owned)
- **Profile:** first-gen couple/solo owner; single taquería/pho/Chinese-takeout/halal-grill/pizzeria; 6–7 days, 11h shifts; owner on the line + register + midnight books. Proud, self-reliant, distrustful of outsiders, cash-conscious. Tech = whoever's youngest/most fluent.
- **Tech maturity (lowest):** basic/legacy POS or cash + Square reader (chose Square for **$0/mo, month-to-month**); often no website or one "the kid set up"; **multiple delivery tablets NOT integrated into POS** ("tablet hell," hand-keyed orders); GBP often unclaimed; **no customer data.** Documented pattern: immigrant-owned businesses rely on teenage/adult children for tech; first-gen owners less likely to see tech as integral. [IND]
- **Buys via:** owner + the **"kid" as de facto IT/translator**; trust flows through **peer owners and ethnic networks (WeChat, ethnic FB groups like "SGV Eats"), not ads**; short but trust-gated cycle; keep demos <15 min. [IND/VENDOR]
- **#1 pain (their words):** **"the apps are eating me alive"** — a $7–8 burrito costs the customer $21 via delivery but nets the restaurant ~$4. Then: **no time** (anything that adds work is DOA); rising costs + fewer patrons; tablet chaos; **English-only contracts/dashboards.** [IND]
- **Budget/WTP:** **$0–$100/mo**, extreme price sensitivity (chose Square *for* $0). Unlocks on **concrete dollar ROI in their terms** ("saves $X/mo in DoorDash commission; pays for itself after 6 direct orders") — show the math on a napkin.
- **Triggers:** app fee hike / payout dispute; a month where the apps' cut erased profit; a competitor getting direct online orders; **the "kid" leaves for college/job** (family IT vanishes); tax/inspection scramble.
- **Objections:** "can't afford another fee / no time / don't trust salespeople / can't read the contract."
- **Reach:** in-person on a slow afternoon, peer introduction, ethnic FB/WeChat groups, **bilingual materials + support** (a differentiator big vendors never offer), no-contract promise.
- **Entry = STOP THE BLEED:** commission-free first-party ordering link + claim/optimize GBP + consolidate delivery tablets into POS. Money *saved*, not spent.

## P2 — "Tony & Angela": single full-service family restaurant
- **Profile:** established independent (Italian-American/diner/steakhouse/sit-down Mexican), 10–25 yrs, local name; a manager + small staff; reservations, real dinner service, maybe catering/events. More business-literate but **no dedicated tech/marketing person.** Proud of reputation, relationship-driven, cautious modernizer.
- **Tech maturity (mid):** real POS (Toast/Square for Restaurants), **under-uses reporting**; dated website; first-party ordering weak/absent (bigger absolute delivery bleed); **cares about reviews but manages them ad hoc**; little/no loyalty (61% of operators offer loyalty; 70% of those saw traffic lift — they're missing a proven tool); inventory/labor in spreadsheets. [IND]
- **Buys via:** owner (+ GM); peers, POS rep, local association, **demonstrable ROI** (scarred by the 83%/28% gap); medium, real-but-short evaluation.
- **#1 pain:** margin compression (labor +10%, food +35%); declining traffic ("where did the regulars go?"); delivery commissions; **"I can't see my numbers"** (gut + lagging spreadsheets); reactive reputation; **repeat revenue left on the table** (no customer database despite loyal regulars). [IND]
- **Budget/WTP:** **$100–$300/mo/loc**, ~2–6% of revenue on tech; moderate sensitivity; **hates lock-in contracts** and vague $1k–$5k/mo "agency on retainer" fees. Unlocks on ROI framed as **covers, average check, repeat visits, labor saved**.
- **Triggers:** a bad quarter; new competitor "everywhere online"; a reputation hit; GM/family member leaving; catering demand outgrowing manual handling.
- **Objections:** "spent on tech before, didn't see it in my P&L"; "no contracts"; "my business is hospitality, not apps"; "agencies are a waste."
- **Reach:** peer referral + ROI-led short demo + local association; speak **P&L and hospitality, not "platform"**; side-by-side proof from a comparable independent; month-to-month.
- **Entry = OPTIMIZE:** repeat-revenue engine — first-party ordering + loyalty/CRM + review management + a simple sales/labor snapshot. (Commission rescue is the on-ramp; optimization is the core sale.)

## P3 — "David": small multi-unit / expanding (2–10 locations)
- **Profile:** proved one concept, now 2–10 units (or a few franchise units); thinks like a small-biz CEO; managers per location but **he IS the ops function**; ambitious, data-curious but data-starved; anxious the one-store model is breaking at three.
- **Tech maturity (mid but FRAGMENTED):** per-location POS instances that **don't roll up**; inconsistent online ordering/loyalty across units; **disconnected systems / data silos**; "head office assembles a group view from emailed spreadsheets"; manual processes "break down at 10 units"; a documented **8–12% EBITDA gap** between best/worst locations no dashboard catches. [IND/SOFT]
- **Buys via:** owner-CEO (+ trusted manager/accountant); peers at his scale, accountant, ROI math; longest cycle of the three but still fast vs. enterprise.
- **#1 pain:** **"I can't see across my locations"** (problems found "at the end of the month"); problems **compound** not add; margin leakage/shrink across many shifts; **owner-dependence at scale** (culture/consistency drift); delivery commissions at volume. [IND/SOFT]
- **Budget/WTP:** **$100–$300+/mo PER location** × units; lower sensitivity *if tied to a number* (food %, labor %, EBITDA gap) — thinks in multiples ("fix 1% across 6 stores").
- **Triggers:** opening location #2–3 (the classic wall); a unit underperforming unnoticed; a manager leaving; inventory shrink surprise; eyeing franchising/capital that demands clean numbers.
- **Objections:** "duct tape sort of works"; "switching is painful/risky"; "no time to roll out across stores"; "will it move EBITDA or is it another dashboard I won't look at?"
- **Reach:** peer multi-unit networks, accountant, CEO-level ROI; language = **EBITDA gap, prime cost %, real-time visibility, consolidation, brand consistency**; same-scale case study; phased one-location-first rollout.
- **Entry = OPTIMIZE → FUTURE-READY:** one dashboard across locations (sales/labor/food cost real-time) + unified ordering/loyalty, then scalable platform/repeatable playbook for the next location. Hook isn't "stop the bleed" — it's **"stop flying blind."**

## Comparison

| | **P1 Mom-and-Pop** | **P2 Full-Service Family** | **P3 Multi-Unit (2–10)** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tech maturity | Lowest (Square/cash, app tablets, no data) | Mid (real POS, under-used; no loyalty/BI) | Mid but FRAGMENTED (silos, spreadsheet roll-ups) |
| Defining gap | Delivery not integrated; no first-party; no English-fluent help | Under-using POS data; no loyalty/CRM; reactive reviews | No cross-location visibility |
| #1 pain | "Apps are eating me alive" | Margin squeeze + falling traffic | "Can't see across locations"; 8–12% EBITDA gap |
| Decision-maker | Owner + the "kid" | Owner (+ GM) | Owner-CEO (+ manager/accountant) |
| Trust signal | Peer owners, ethnic FB/WeChat, bilingual rep | Peers, POS rep, ROI proof | Peer multi-unit operators, accountant, ROI math |
| Budget/WTP | $0–$100/mo, extreme | $100–$300/mo/loc, moderate, hates contracts | $100–$300+/mo/loc × units, ROI-justifiable |
| Top trigger | App fee hike / "kid" leaves / competitor online | Bad quarter / new competitor / rep hit | Opening loc #2–3 / a unit underperforms unnoticed |
| Entry point | **STOP THE BLEED** | **OPTIMIZE** | **OPTIMIZE → FUTURE-READY** |

## Highest-ROI / lowest-effort wedge per persona
- **P1:** "**Commission Rescue + Tablet Consolidation**" — commission-free ordering + GBP + consolidate tablets, **bilingual, month-to-month, pitched as dollars saved.**
- **P2:** "**Repeat-Revenue Engine**" — first-party ordering + loyalty/CRM + review management + sales/labor snapshot; trackable covers/repeat visits to counter the "tech never showed in my P&L" scar.
- **P3:** "**One Dashboard, All Locations**" — cross-location BI (sales/labor/food cost) + unified ordering/loyalty; ROI = close the 8–12% EBITDA gap; land one location, prove, roll out.
