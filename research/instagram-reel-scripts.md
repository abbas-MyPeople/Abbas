A ready-to-shoot starter set for a **new Instagram account**, plus a short channel strategy. Built for the founder's real voice — an operator from a restaurant family, not a slick agency.

## The goal & how this channel works

**Goal.** Top-of-funnel awareness. We're not trying to close anyone in a reel — we're trying to make an independent restaurant owner (or their kid, their GM, their friend) stop scrolling, feel *seen*, and think "this guy actually gets it." Every reel quietly drives to **azrestaurantpartners.com** and the **free call** (and the free 2-minute toolbox finder at `azrestaurantpartners.com/toolbox.html`).

**Who we're talking to.** Independent, family-run restaurants in Greater Houston — takeout/counter spots, single full-service places, and small 2–10 unit operators. Their real pain, in their words:
- "The apps are eating me alive" — losing **15–30% of every order** to delivery commissions, and the apps own the customer.
- Drowning in disconnected tools and tablets that don't talk to each other.
- Missing takeout and reservation calls in the rush.
- Can't see their real numbers until month-end.
- Reviews stuck or slipping; getting harder to find online.
- Feeling like they're one person against a system built for the big chains.

**Voice rules.** Warm, plain-English, operator-to-operator. Honest. Confident but never hypey. No jargon, no "10X your revenue," no fake urgency. Abbas talks like a guy who's stood on the line, because he has — his own place, **Wok & Karahi in Spring, TX**, has been profitable every year it's been open. When in doubt, say the true, specific thing.

**Cadence.** Start with **3–4 reels a week** for the first 6–8 weeks while you find which hooks land. Reuse the formats that work; quietly retire the ones that don't. Consistency beats polish — a phone, daylight, and a real face out-perform a glossy edit every time.

**Test organically before you spend a dollar.** This account is also your cheapest ad lab. Post hooks here for free, watch the first-3-second retention and saves/shares, and only *then* put money behind the 2–3 that already proved they stop the scroll. Never boost a hook the organic audience ignored. The same "prove it first, pay second" logic we sell to owners is how we run our own marketing.

## Production notes

**Filming (solo founder, just a phone):**
- Shoot vertical (9:16), lock your phone against something or use a $15 tripod. Stillness reads as "professional" more than any filter.
- Face a window. Natural daylight on your face beats any ring light. Avoid harsh overhead kitchen lights that shadow your eyes.
- Record audio in a quiet moment (before open, after close). Phone mic is fine if there's no background clatter; a clip-on lav mic ($20) is a worthwhile upgrade.
- Nail the first 3 seconds. If you fumble the hook, restart — the hook is 80% of the result. Everything after just has to keep the promise.
- Use real settings: the line, the pass, the dining room, a takeout bag, the POS, the missed-calls screen. Authenticity is your whole edge over agencies — lean into it. It's fine to be a little rough.
- Keep most reels 25–35 seconds. Shorter watches all the way through; full watch-through is what the algorithm rewards.
- Batch it: film 3–4 reels in one sitting so you're not "making content" every day.

**Captions / SEO:**
- Front-load the caption with the hook line — only the first ~125 characters show before "more."
- Put your location in your bio and tag it on every post (Spring TX / Greater Houston). You want to surface for local owners.
- Always burn on-screen text into the video — most people watch muted. Use Instagram's auto-captions too.
- Reuse a tight, varied hashtag set: mix local (#houstonrestaurants #htxeats #spring #thewoodlands #tomball) with owner/operator tags (#restaurantowner #independentrestaurant #restaurantmarketing). Skip giant generic tags like #food — too crowded to rank.
- Pin your best-performing reel and your Founding 5 reel (Reel 8) to the top of the grid.
- Bio link: point it to **azrestaurantpartners.com** (or a link-in-bio tool listing the site + the free toolbox finder + the free call).

**Tracking which reel drove site visits:**
- The site already captures **UTM parameters**, so tag every link you post so visits get attributed. Use a distinct campaign name per reel:
  - `azrestaurantpartners.com/?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=reel&utm_campaign=burrito-math` (Reel 1)
  - `azrestaurantpartners.com/?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=reel&utm_campaign=missed-calls` (Reel 2)
  - `azrestaurantpartners.com/?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=reel&utm_campaign=pos-regret` (Reel 3)
  - `azrestaurantpartners.com/?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=reel&utm_campaign=no-shows` (Reel 4)
  - `azrestaurantpartners.com/?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=reel&utm_campaign=reviews-revenue` (Reel 5)
  - `azrestaurantpartners.com/?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=reel&utm_campaign=see-your-numbers` (Reel 6)
  - `azrestaurantpartners.com/?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=reel&utm_campaign=toolbox` (Reel 7 — point this one at `/toolbox.html` with the same UTMs)
  - `azrestaurantpartners.com/?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=reel&utm_campaign=founding-5` (Reel 8)
- Keep `utm_source=instagram` and `utm_medium=reel` constant; only change `utm_campaign`. That tells you exactly which reel earned the click, not just that "Instagram worked."
- Read it together with the in-app numbers: organic signal = **3-second retention, saves, and shares** (saves/shares matter more than likes). The reel that drives the most *site visits* AND saves is your first candidate to put ad budget behind.
