**Prepared for:** AZ Integrations
**Scope:** US · the structural patterns reshaping *all* industries, the restaurant lag on each, the payments/fintech shift, and what operators are actually saying · history → **now (2025–2026)** → trajectory
**Date:** June 2026

## 0. The single thesis

Industries are **converging on the same handful of patterns**, and restaurants are a trailing adopter on almost all of them. Nearly every lag traces to **one root cause: restaurants don't own the customer relationship or its data** — they ceded it to delivery aggregators. That one gap cascades into weak personalization, blunt subscriptions, no embedded finance, and total absence from the next front door (AI agents). The sectors that converged fastest (retail, finance, travel, SaaS) all did so *on top of owned customer data*. **Where other industries already are tells us where restaurants are going — and the winners will treat owning the customer, not closing the transaction, as the core asset.**

## 1. The elite cross-industry patterns (and restaurant lag)

Ranked most-behind → least.

### Pattern 2 — Embedded finance / BNPL / split pay · **LAG: most severe**
- **Cross-industry now:** finance is dissolving into every transaction surface. BNPL heading to ~$576B by 2026 [IND]; **Klarna** posted its first $1B+ quarter (Q4'25 rev +38%), serves 29M US consumers, and its *banking* users (3× revenue each) doubled in a year [IND, SEC]. Amazon/Walmart integrated BNPL at checkout [IND].
- **Restaurants:** adopted only the *easy* half — at-table **split payments** (Toast Mobile Order & Pay) — and almost none of the hard half. **BNPL on a restaurant check is essentially absent in the US at scale**; no restaurant Klarna, no restaurant-as-banking-surface. Restaurants treat payment as a transaction to close, not a financial relationship to own — the opposite of where finance is converging. [VENDOR, Toast; IND]

### Patterns 5 & 6 (external) — Agentic commerce / super-apps · **LAG: severe**
- **The buying interface itself is being rebuilt around AI agents.** ChatGPT Instant Checkout (+Stripe, Sept 2025, 900M weekly users); Visa+OpenAI tokenized agent checkout; Mastercard Agent Pay; Google AP2 (Mastercard, PayPal, Amex, Shopify, Etsy). Agentic commerce projected **~$1T US retail by 2030** [IND]. Uber is bundling OpenTable/Expedia into a Western **super-app** [IND].
- **Restaurants are absent** — and the risk is that delivery apps (already the data owners) become the agent-readable layer, deepening disintermediation. **This is the next disintermediation wave after delivery apps — the one most likely to catch the industry flat-footed.**

### Pattern 1 — Disintermediation / going direct · **LAG: moderate–severe**
- **The clearest analogy.** Third-party delivery (DoorDash 15–30% headline; **30–40%+ all-in**) is restaurants' Amazon; direct ordering costs only ~2.9–3.3%. Shifting 30% of delivery to direct can recover **$40k+/yr** for a 1,000-order/mo independent [VENDOR/IND].
- The 2026 retail playbook — *use marketplaces to acquire, pull repeat customers to owned channels for margin + data* — is exactly what restaurants should do, but they're running it **~5 years late** and still **hand their customer data to competitors** [IND, PYMNTS/eMarketer]. Complication: first-party delivery has **lost its price edge** ($5.96 vs $5.14 third-party fees in 2025), so restaurants must win on *relationship*, not price [IND].

### Pattern 5 (internal) — AI agents / automation · **LAG: moderate**
- Enterprises: Gartner says **40% of enterprise apps will embed task-specific AI agents by end-2026** (from <5%) [IND]. Restaurants have *real but narrow* deployments — Wendy's FreshAI (−22s, +15% upsell), Yum! AI drive-thru at 300+ Taco Bells, Byte by Yum! SaaS — but voice AI (~85% accuracy) still trails humans (89–92%) [IND]. Single-task agents, not the multi-agent systems enterprises run. **Note caution:** Gartner expects >40% of agentic projects cancelled by 2027 [IND].

### Pattern 7 — Dynamic pricing / data-as-moat · **LAG: moderate**
- Retail is installing the rails (electronic shelf labels: Walmart all US stores by end-2026) [IND]. Restaurants tried to leap to **surge pricing and got burned** (Wendy's 2024 backlash) — hitting the same **consumer-trust wall** that gates grocery. The deeper gap is the **missing first-party data foundation** everything else depends on. [IND]

### Pattern 4 — Subscriptions / membership · **LAG: mild**
- Subscription economy ~$623B (2025) → $739B (2026) [IND]. Restaurants are *on-trend*: **Panera Sip Club** reached **25% of transactions**; Sweetgreen, Taco Bell, P.F. Chang's piloting [IND]. But mostly **blunt single-product passes**, not the AI-driven, retention-optimized membership the broader economy is converging on. In the room, using a 2018-era version.

### Pattern 3 — Decentralization / Web3 · **LAG: none (correctly skipped)**
- Tokenized loyalty failed as consumer tech (**Starbucks Odyssey shut March 2024**); survives only as hidden infrastructure [IND]. Restaurants correctly avoided the NFT hype, and their *own* decentralization experiment — **ghost kitchens** — ran ahead and **collapsed** (projected >20% of revenue by 2025; instead "most gone or pivoting") [IND]. The open opportunity: hidden-blockchain loyalty with real utility — which nobody has cracked.

## 2. Payments & fintech evolution in restaurants

The convergence pattern restated at the register, drawn from the cross-industry, wallets, payouts, crypto, and tipping streams plus social listening.

**Contactless / wallets — past the tipping point.** Contactless is **~60% of US in-store payments** (Mastercard's network >75%); **~28% of POS** is a phone/watch wallet tap [IND]. **Tap-to-Pay on phone** (softPOS) is the fastest-growing acceptance vector (Visa +200% YoY), great for pop-ups/food-trucks/line-busting [VENDOR]. **Branded app wallets are the "restaurant-as-quasi-bank" play:** **Starbucks holds ~$1.8–2.1B of customer money** parked in its app (interest-free float, ~$207M/yr breakage); Rewards drive ~59% of US sales [VENDOR/SEC]. *This is the embedded-finance + owned-customer pattern done right — and it's available to anyone with a loyalty + stored-value program.*

**BNPL / split / pay-later.** Split-the-bill at table is solved (Toast); **pay-later on the check is essentially absent** in US restaurants — the clearest open gap vs. every other retail surface (see Pattern 2).

**Surcharging / dual pricing — mainstream and contentious.** **~34% of merchants added card surcharges in 2024**; consumers actively resist ("3% fee… the new normal?") and regulators are policing disclosure (NJ cited 16 establishments) [SOFT]. A margin tactic that trades goodwill — handle with disclosure care.

**Embedded lending / faster money.** Thin margins (**42% of operators unprofitable**) spawned a "get money faster" category: **instant settlement** (Square 1.75%; SpotOn Rapid Fund undercutting Toast), **earned-wage-access / instant tips** as a retention tool amid 130%+ turnover (Toast PayOut, Kickfin, 7shifts→Clair), and **data-underwritten capital** (Toast Capital, DoorDash Capital — sales-based, no credit check) [IND/VENDOR]. Regulatory tailwind: **Dec 2025 CFPB ruling that earned-wage-access isn't credit** [IND].

**Tipping.** Backlash rising fast (**41%** "out of control" per Bankrate; ~90% per WalletHub; **42% favor banning** tips), but actual tip rates only drift down (Toast full-service ~19.1%). Two 2025–26 twists: the **"No Tax on Tips" federal law disqualifies mandatory service charges** (pushing restaurants back toward voluntary tips after the service-charge surge), and **DC/Chicago rolled back tip-credit elimination** [IND]. Instant tip-payout tech is consolidating into earned-wage-access.

**Crypto / stablecoin — hype vs. reality.** Consumer crypto-at-checkout is **<2% of US payments and *falling*** [IND, Kansas City Fed]. The real momentum is **settlement/payout rails** (Visa USDC settlement; DoorDash/Tempo merchant payouts; Square rolling 0%-fee Bitcoin to ~4M sellers). Steak 'n Shake (Bitcoin via Lightning, ~50% lower processing fees) is the standout case but promotional. **Not actionable for clients today — useful to *know* so you sound credible.**

## 3. What operators are actually saying (social listening)

> **Honest limit:** Reddit/X/Facebook were largely walled off to web tools; themes below are well-corroborated across secondary sources, but verbatim community quotes are thin. A paid social tool would be needed to harden quotes.

1. **Margin panic underlies everything.** 42% unprofitable; every gripe (commissions, POS fees, surcharging, catering) is a hunt for margin points. [SOFT/IND, NRA]
2. **"Burned once, cautious now" on tech.** The McDonald's AI drive-thru failure + Toast's 2025 fee/contract anger created a defensive posture — operators want **measurable, low-risk, no-lock-in, exitable** tools. [SOFT]
3. **Phone-order AI beats drive-thru AI** as the practical wedge (~30% of calls go unanswered at peak; ROI is easy to isolate). Back-of-house AI has more goodwill than guest-facing. [SOFT]
4. **POS gripes center on Toast** — rate hikes, **2–3yr contracts + ETFs**, hardware lock-in, per-feature nickel-and-diming. "Commission-free" SaaS (Owner.com, Popmenu) draws mixed-to-skeptical reviews. [SOFT]
5. **Consultant distrust is real** ("snake oil"). What flips it: **systems not slide decks**, executable/teach-me handoffs, and **clear attributable ROI**. Same trust currency as tech. [SOFT]
6. **Delivery commissions** are the #1 villain; **going direct** is the most-cited escape, with markup-on-apps and flat-fee courier as workarounds. [SOFT]

## 4. Synthesis — where restaurants are heading & what it means for AZ

**The destination is predictable** because the convergence points are clear:
1. A hard push to **reclaim direct customer relationships + first-party data** (the prerequisite for everything else).
2. **AI-personalized membership/subscriptions** replacing blunt passes.
3. **Embedded finance at the check** (pay-later, split, restaurant-branded wallets/stored value) once someone productizes it — Starbucks already proves the float.
4. Restaurants forced to become **agent-readable and agent-ordered** as AI agents become the buying layer — the next disintermediation wave.
5. **Algorithmic/dynamic pricing** via digital menu boards, gated by the consumer-trust wall.

**What this means for AZ Integrations' positioning (all evidence-backed):**
- **Lead with "own your customer, not just your transaction."** It's the single root fix and it underpins delivery→direct, loyalty/data, and future-proofing against AI agents. This is your north star *and* the client's.
- **Sell to the margin panic.** Frame every engagement as attributable margin recovery (delivery→direct $40k+/yr, catering's 2–3× margins, missed-call capture) — that's what 42%-unprofitable operators and skeptical Redditors actually pay for.
- **Beat consultant distrust by design:** transparent pricing, **systems you build and hand off**, isolated ROI, **no lock-in** — the exact opposite of the Toast/Popmenu grievances. This *is* your "operator-led enabler, not a software vendor" wedge.
- **Phone-order AI is the safe first wedge**, not flashy drive-thru AI — easy ROI, low brand risk, matches operator sentiment.
- **Catering + delivery→direct are the two highest-ROI, lowest-controversy plays** to lead with (see the catering report).
- **Know the frontier (agentic commerce, embedded finance, stablecoin rails) even where it's not yet actionable** — it's how you out-credential every other "restaurant tech" vendor when an owner tests you.
