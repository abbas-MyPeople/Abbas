# The Decentralization of Power — Cross-Industry Investigation & Restaurant Implications

**Thesis:** Across the economy, capability and economic power once locked inside large centralized control centers (gatekeepers, networks, incumbents) are being pushed to the "edge" — individuals and small businesses. **But a counter-current runs alongside: aggregator platforms re-capture the edge and re-concentrate control over data, customers, and economics.** The honest picture is a *standing tension*, not a clean arc. Restaurants are a textbook case of both forces at once.

> NOT a crypto thesis — crypto is a tiny sub-instance. Tags: **[IND]** independent/academic/press · **[VENDOR]** company · **[SOFT]** blog.

## Part 1 — The pattern, domain by domain (history → now → trajectory)

- **Media / creator economy:** was centralized in studios/labels/networks. Now ~**$250B (2025)**, and **individual creators generated 58.7% of revenue by end-user share** — not agencies. Branded creator content "exceeded traditional digital ads in virtually all categories." [IND, Grand View] *Strongly decentralizing on capability and revenue share.*
- **Retail:** big-box shelf-space gatekeepers → **Shopify ~$378B GMV (2025), +29%, ~6.2% of global e-commerce**, "maintaining brand ownership and customer relationships." But concentration persists: **1% of merchants = 51% of GMV.** [IND, Shopify SEC/ECDB] *Tools democratized; outcomes power-law.*
- **Finance (fintech, not crypto):** banks → embedded finance. **Square Capital advanced $9B+ to 460,000 merchants**; US embedded credit **$7.65B (2024) → $45.7B (2034)**; **banks now hold ~38% of global financial assets vs ~49% for non-banks** (FSB). [IND]
- **Software / AI — the engine:** IT departments & frontier labs → no-code + AI agents give one person team-scale capability (solopreneur stacks $15–50/mo). **DeepSeek-R1 (Jan 2025) matched OpenAI o1 at a fraction of cost**; Llama/Qwen/Gemma/Mistral ship open weights; distilled models run on a single RTX 4090 / M4 Max. [IND] *The single most powerful decentralizing force — it powers all the others.*
- **Labor / work:** employers → **~70M Americans (~36%) in the gig/independent economy**; freelancers ~$1.3T; **5.6M independents earn $100K+**; AI-skill demand +109% YoY on Upwork (40%+ premium); 70% independent by choice. [IND]
- **Manufacturing:** factories → on-demand 3D printing / micro-manufacturing ("weeks to hours"); e.g., 3D Systems + Daimler printing ~40,000 bus spare parts on demand. *Real but capital-bound; mass commodity stays centralized.* [IND]
- **Energy:** utilities → rooftop solar + batteries + VPPs. **Sunrun dispatched 535 MW from 100,000+ CA homes (Jul 29, 2025) — half of SF's demand**; Puerto Rico distributed solar 81% of new capacity 2016–25. *Capability real; scale lags.* [IND]
- **Healthcare/education:** telehealth ~**23% of US encounters**; at-home diagnostics; individual experts monetize teaching directly. (Note Oct 2025 telehealth reimbursement rollbacks — institutions push back.) [IND]

## Part 2 — The counter-force: re-centralization (be honest)

Ben Thompson's **Aggregation Theory**: in the internet era the winners don't control *supply*, they control *demand*. Once an aggregator owns the end-user relationship, suppliers "come onto the platform on the aggregator's terms, effectively commoditizing and modularizing themselves." **Platforms** empower third parties to reach users directly; **aggregators** intermediate and control the relationship. That distinction is the whole game. [IND/SOFT, Stratechery]

Evidence of re-centralization: **Amazon ~35.7% of US e-commerce**; **<8,000 sellers now generate half of Amazon's ~$300B 3P GMV (down from ~15,000 three years earlier)**; Amazon began charging 3P developers **$1,400/yr + usage** (2026); Google/Meta ad duopoly still owns intent + feed; app stores' ~30% tax. **The tension in one sentence: tools decentralize capability while platforms re-centralize control, data, and economics.** [IND, Marketplace Pulse/PPC.land] The decisive new variable: **AI (open weights + agents) is the first force in years pushing power out faster than platforms can recentralize it**, by collapsing the cost of the capability that justified the platform's cut.

## Part 3 — Apply to restaurants

**How power was centralized away from operators** — a near-perfect Aggregation case: **DoorDash ~67% / Uber Eats ~23%** of US food delivery; DoorDash alone **$102B GOV across 615,000 merchants (2025)**. The apps capture the **money** (15–30% headline, **40%+ all-in** vs **3–9% net margins** — "structurally impossible"), the **customer** ("guest data on DoorDash/Uber/Instagram doesn't belong to you"), and **pricing/discovery**. [IND/SOFT]

**The decentralization opportunity (now):** (1) reclaiming the **direct relationship + owned data** — commission-free direct ordering; **CDP/first-party-data investment +11% YoY, outpacing loyalty spend for the first time**; (2) **AI capability that used to require a big chain** — **voice-AI adoption hit 34% of restaurants (2025)** and **independents are adopting voice agents faster than large chains.** [VENDOR/IND]

**Where restaurants sit vs. other industries:** on *capability decentralization* — early-but-accelerating (roughly where retail/Shopify was a few years into D2C; ahead on independent voice-AI). On *control re-centralization* — among the **most captured** industries in the economy. **The gap between "capability available" and "control reclaimed" is unusually wide — that gap is the arbitrage.**

## Part 4 — Synthesis

Real and durable **for capability**; contested **for control**. Overstated when told as a clean arc (aggregators systematically re-capture the edge), accurate as a **standing tension** decided by *who owns demand and data*. For restaurants — the most captured industry — the value of reclaiming the direct relationship is unusually high. The winning posture is **not** "leave the platforms" (they own real demand) but **"own the high-value layer"**: the direct channel, the first-party data/CDP, and AI that gives one location chain-level operating leverage. Use aggregators as paid acquisition; convert that traffic into owned relationships.

**The strategic warning that must shape AZ:** *whoever owns the operator's customer and data ends up owning the operator.* A mission to put capability back in independents' hands must itself be a **platform (empowering; data stays with the operator), not an aggregator** — or it becomes the next thing extracting 30%. The durable moat = **operator-owned demand + operator-owned data + AI leverage, delivered as a tool, not an intermediary.**
