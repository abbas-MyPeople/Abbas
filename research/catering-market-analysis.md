# US Restaurant Catering & Off-Premise Market — Strategy Report

**Scope:** United States · history → **now (2025–2026)** → trajectory · all catering segments
**Date:** June 2026

---

## 0. One-paragraph takeaway

Off-premise is now the structural core of US restaurants — **38% of sales (~$199B) and ~75% of all traffic** [IND, NRA]. Within it, **catering is the highest-margin slice you can build**: average catering orders run **~$280–$500 vs. $20–40 dine-in** [SOFT], net margins **~7–15% vs. 3–6% dine-in** [IND], and **corporate/workplace catering is the fastest-growing piece (~7–9% CAGR)** on the 2025 return-to-office wave [IND/VENDOR]. With **42% of operators unprofitable in 2026** [IND], catering's margin premium is strategically central. Your existing Uber courier setup already puts you on the right (commission-free, flat-fee) delivery rail — the gap is building a deliberate catering *channel* around it.

## 1. Market size, growth & why catering matters

**History → now → trajectory:** pre-2020 off-premise was a minority of sales and catering was a fragmented, under-digitized "$60B+ opportunity" [VENDOR, ezCater 2019]. COVID forced an off-premise shift that never reverted. **Now: off-premise = 38% of restaurant sales = $198.7B, ~75% of all traffic**, growing faster than overall sales [IND, NRA "From Trend to Transformation," 2025]. Total US foodservice ~**$1.55T in 2026** [IND, NRA].

**US catering market size — honest range** (firms define "catering" differently):

| Source | Type | Base | Projection | CAGR |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Expert Market Research | [IND] | $77.2B (2025) | $140.9B by 2035 | 6.2% |
| US Contract Catering (ResearchAndMarkets) | [IND] | $73.3B (2024) | $110.2B by 2030 | 7.0% |
| Grand View Research | [IND] | — | $109.4B by 2030 | 7.7% |
| MarknTel Advisors | [IND] | ~$55B (2024) | ~$71B by 2030 | ~5.2% |

→ **~$55–77B today → ~$71–140B by 2030–35, CAGR clustering 5–8%** [IND]. (Higher figures include institutional/contract catering.)

**Corporate vs. social:** social/consumer (weddings, parties) ≈ 63%; corporate/institutional ≈ 37% — but **corporate grows faster (~8.8% CAGR for workplace)** [IND]. **53%+ of corporate buyers planned to raise catering budgets in 2025**; **43% of orgs now run a recurring meal program, +17% YoY** [VENDOR, ezCater].

**Why catering wins on economics (quantified):**
- **AOV is the driver:** ~$360 (ChowNow), $283 typical / ~$500 first-party (Lunchbox), $420 avg workplace order ~25 headcount (ezCater) — **8–10× a takeout ticket** [SOFT/VENDOR].
- **Net margin ~7–15% vs. dine-in 3–5%** — ~2–3× more profitable [IND, DoorDash/R365/UpMenu].
- **Mechanism:** similar COGS, but catering avoids much fixed **labor/occupancy** burden — **batched & planned** (24–48h lead), prepped in **off-peak hours** (monetizes idle kitchen), **prepaid/predictable**, **buy-to-order** (less waste) [SOFT, Toast/ezCater].
- Restaurants with catering grew revenue **5.1% vs. 3.3% industry avg** [VENDOR, ezCater citing Technomic].

## 2. Segments & economics (easiest/highest-margin → hardest)

| Segment | Typical AOV | Net margin | Labor | Lead time | Fit for independent |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Party trays / family packs** | $40–$200/tray | Strong (standardized, batch) | Minimal | Same-day–24h | **Easiest entry** |
| **Drop-off / delivery** | $100–$500+ ($15–25/pp) | **Highest** (no service labor) | Kitchen + 1 driver | 24–48h | **Best core channel** |
| **Corporate/office lunch (recurring)** | $200–$800/occasion | Excellent; recurring | Kitchen + delivery | 24–48h | **Growth engine / "holy grail"** |
| **Events / private** | $1K–$15K+ ($30–100/pp) | Variable (service erodes) | Higher, planning-heavy | 1–8 wks | Lumpy, seasonal |
| **Full-service (staff/setup)** | $50–$150/pp; $2K–$20K+ | Gross high, **net compressed** | Heavy (1 server/10–20) | Weeks–months | Hardest |
| **Weddings/social** | $5K–$50K+ | Net compressed; referral-driven | Heaviest | Months–year | Specialist only |

[Economics: SOFT/VENDOR — ChowNow, Lunchbox, Toast, Square; IND for margin norms]

**Pricing norms** [SOFT/IND]: per-person ($12–25 office; $30–150 events); **service charge 15–24%** (≈18–20%, separate from gratuity); **gratuity 15–20%**; delivery $10–50+ flat; **food cost target ~28–35%** (≈3× markup); **minimums $100–250** drop-off; **deposits 25–50%** for events/weddings.

**Recommendation:** start with **party trays + drop-off corporate lunch** — highest margin, lowest operational risk, leverages your kitchen + Uber courier. Avoid full-service/weddings unless you build a dedicated events team (service labor eats the margin; execution risk is high).

## 4. Logistics — couriers, white-label & large orders

**White-label / commission-free courier options** [VENDOR/IND]:
- **Uber Direct** — your branding, flat fee ~**$7–15** (varies by distance/market), handles large orders, integrates via POS/API. Aligns with your current Uber usage.
- **DoorDash Drive** — flat ~$6–10+ base; most-developed **dedicated catering/large-order program** (catering-trained Dashers + **setup** in many markets). Often stronger for true large catering.
- **Roadie** (UPS) — crowdsourced, broader vehicles for bulky loads. **Nash / Burq** — orchestration layers routing across many couriers for best price/backup. **Relay** — flat-fee last-mile in select metros.

**The economics that validate your approach:** on a $500 catering order, flat **$10–15 = ~2–3%** vs. 15–30% marketplace = **$75–150** [IND]. White-label is dramatically cheaper for high-value orders.

**Large-order challenges** [IND/VENDOR]: gig sedans can't fit multiple trays/hot boxes (need SUV/van or multi-driver); **temperature control mandatory** — hot ≥140°F, cold ≤40°F, the **40–140°F danger zone** breeds bacteria, discard after **2h** (FDA/USDA/ServSafe); corporate lunches are **time-critical** (late = lost account); many need **setup** (gig drivers usually don't).

**Equipment** [IND]: insulated bags, **Cambro/hot boxes**, chafing dishes + sterno, cooler bags/ice; transport hot & cold separately; non-slip racks; minimize transit.

**The fix — hybrid model:** **own van + dedicated, food-safety-trained driver for big/important corporate orders** (reliability, branding, temp, setup, no per-order fee) **+ on-demand courier (Uber Direct) for overflow & smaller drop-offs** [IND]. Add the dedicated driver as anchor-account volume grows.

## 6. Trends now (2025–2026)

1. **Return-to-office is the dominant tailwind.** Federal RTO order (Jan 2025, ~2.3M federal workers); Amazon 5-day (Jan 2025); JPMorgan/AT&T/Dell mandates; **~33% of companies required 5 days in-office by 2025** [IND]. Companies use **catered meals as an attendance magnet** ("Free Food Friday," **Tue–Thu anchor-day** catering concentrates midweek demand) [VENDOR/IND].
2. **Individually-packaged / single-serve** = corporate default (hygiene, portioning, dietary, contactless); buffet trays returned only for social events [IND].
3. **Group ordering** (each employee picks) converging with individual packaging as the dominant corporate model [VENDOR/IND].
4. **Ghost/virtual catering brands:** post-2023–24 shakeout, virtual catering-only concepts are a capital-efficient add-on using idle kitchen capacity [IND].
5. **Subscription / recurring office meal programs** expanding (ezCater Relish; 43% of orgs run one, +17% YoY) [VENDOR].
6. **Sustainability:** compostable/recyclable packaging + waste reduction (NRA "What's Hot," plastic bans) — in tension with single-serve waste; compostable single-serve is the compromise [IND].
7. **Dietary & customization:** allergen labeling, vegan/GF/halal/kosher, per-person personalization now baseline [IND].
8. **AI** (forecasting, personalization, dynamic pricing, chatbots) [IND/VENDOR].
9. **Employee meal stipends/benefits** a fast-growing budget line (recruitment/retention/RTO compliance) via Sharebite/ezCater/DoorDash/Uber/Grubhub for Business [VENDOR/IND].

## 7. Pain points & the build playbook

**What's hard for independents** [IND/SOFT/VENDOR]: capacity conflict with regular service; large-batch labor; execution risk (one big-order error hits many guests; orders must be 100% complete); hot/cold transport + packaging cost; corporate invoicing/net-terms/tax-exempt/house-accounts admin; B2B lead-gen skills most kitchens lack.

**Why they under-invest / common mistakes:** treat catering as a reactive side-business; fear of disrupting core service; unaware of margins. Mistakes: **using the regular menu** (items that don't travel/hold), **no minimums/lead-times/capacity caps**, **underpricing** (ignoring packaging/delivery/labor), **poor delivery execution**, **not capturing customer data for repeat business** [SOFT/IND].

**Step-by-step build plan (tailored to you):**
1. **Dedicated catering menu** — only items that travel/hold/scale; individually-packaged options + packages (per-person, family-style); vegan/GF (+ halal/kosher for local employers).
2. **Guardrails** — minimums ($100–250), lead times (24–48h), cutoffs, capacity caps, blackout dates. Protect core service.
3. **Packaging & delivery** — insulated/branded/sustainable packaging; **keep Uber Direct for drop-offs**; add a **dedicated driver/van for big anchor accounts** as volume grows.
4. **Price for profit** — food + packaging + delivery + labor + service/delivery fees; ~30% food cost target.
5. **Channel strategy** — list on **ezCater for discovery**; build a **direct catering page** (Toast/Square/CaterZen/ChowNow) and migrate repeat clients to direct.
6. **CRM + a catering sales owner** — capture every account, track history, automate reorder follow-ups; assign one person to corporate accounts. **Highest-LTV prize.**
7. **Marketing** — Google Business Profile/local SEO, direct corporate outreach, **office tastings/sampling**, mine existing dine-in guests (they plan events).
8. **Capitalize on RTO now** — pitch **recurring Tue–Thu office lunch programs** + individually-packaged meals to nearby employers.

**Benchmarks (directional — flagged gaps):** catering can become **20–30%+ of revenue** when built as a dedicated channel [SOFT/IND]; ramp is relationship-based (months); add a catering coordinator as volume grows. **Independent attach/repeat/LTV benchmarks are scarce in public data — track internally.**

## Takeaways for AZ Integrations (as the operator AND as a service you can sell clients)
1. **You're already on the right delivery rail** — Uber Direct/courier (~2–3% of a $500 order) vs. 15–30% marketplace. Lean in for direct; add a dedicated van/driver for big anchor accounts; consider DoorDash Drive's trained-catering program for setup-heavy orders.
2. **"Build a catering channel" is itself a high-value service offering** for your clients — dedicated menu, guardrails, profit pricing, ezCater-for-discovery → direct-for-repeat, CRM + a catering sales motion. Most independents under-invest here despite 2–3× margins.
3. **Sell the 2025–26 trend** — recurring, individually-packaged, midweek office-lunch programs to local employers bringing staff back.
