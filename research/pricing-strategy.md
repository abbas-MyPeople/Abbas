# Pricing Strategy — AZ Restaurant Partners (fresh build, June 2026)

Built from scratch against (a) what comparable providers actually charge, (b) our 3 buyer types' real willingness-to-pay, and (c) our non-negotiables: **no vendor commissions**, **you pay after you save**, **Founding 5 a bit cheaper and locked for life — regular is regular.**

---

## 1) TL;DR — what I recommend

Charge in **four ways**, used in combination, never just one:

1. **Setup / build fee** — one-time, per fix. Covers the work. De-risks us, and clients understand it.
2. **Monthly plan ("your fractional tech & strategy team")** — recurring, tiered by size. The long-term relationship and our LTV.
3. **Savings guarantee, not a savings split** — the monthly is backed by a money-back-if-you-don't-save promise. (We tried "% of savings forever" on paper — the research says attribution fights kill it. Better: flat monthly + guarantee.)
4. **Contingency recovery** — for clawing back what the apps owe: we take a **cut of money actually recovered**, nothing if we recover nothing.

**The anchor line for every conversation:** a marketing agency wants **$1,000–$5,000/mo** and a fractional exec **$5,000–$20,000/mo** — for advice. We're the team that does the work, for a **fraction of one of those**, and you only keep paying because it's paying for itself.

## 2) What the market actually charges (so we can price against it)

| What | Typical market price | Source takeaway |
|---|---|---|
| Restaurant/local **marketing agency retainer** | **$1,000–$5,000/mo** (avg ~$1,800–$6,000; local 2026 $2,500–$10,000+) | This is the thing our buyers got burned by. We are the accessible alternative. |
| **Fractional CMO/CTO** | **$5,000–$20,000/mo** (avg ~$10–12k); $200–500/hr | "Big-chain capability" normally costs this. Our value anchor. |
| **Reputation / review management** | software **$99–$599/loc/mo**; managed **$149–$249/mo** | One play's market price — useful as a per-feature reference. |
| **Delivery reconciliation / loss recovery** | SaaS (quote-only); recovers **up to ~21% of lost revenue**, reclaims up to 80% of disputed fees | Justifies a contingency model on chargeback recovery. |
| **Performance/rev-share pricing** | varies | Disarms the risk objection, easier to sell — but pure rev-share is rare; attribution is the trap; **hybrids win**. → setup + flat retainer + guarantee. |
| **Productized setup + retainer** | e.g. $4k setup → $3k/mo (generic agency) | Validated best practice: *project fee to launch, retainer once it's live and paying for itself.* Exactly our shape — at lower numbers. |
| **What independents actually budget** | **3–8% of revenue** on marketing; 2–6% on tech | A $700k/yr place has only ~$1,200–$3,600/mo for tech+marketing combined. Our plans must fit a *slice* of that. |

**The strategic gap we own:** big-chain capability is priced for big chains ($5–20k/mo) or sold as retainer advice that didn't move the P&L ($1–5k/mo). Nobody prices the *whole team, done-for-you,* for an independent's budget with a savings guarantee. That's our lane.

## 3) The four ways to charge — and when to use each

### A. Setup / build fee (one-time) — **always**
Productized, fixed price per fix, quoted before any work. This is our cash engine and covers labor regardless of outcome. Setup-to-launch, then move them onto a monthly plan once it's live — the research-backed path.

### B. Monthly plan (recurring) — **the core**
Three tiers (below). This is "the tech team you could never hire," billed monthly, month-to-month, cancel anytime. Backed by the savings guarantee so it never feels like a gamble.

### C. Savings guarantee (not a split) — **the de-risker**
We estimate the savings up front and **guarantee them in writing**: if a strategy engagement doesn't put the promised money back within the agreed window, you don't pay the strategy fee (or we work free until it does). Cleaner than a forever revenue-share, and it kills the "tech never showed in my P&L" objection. Tracked transparently from the POS and the apps — no spreadsheets, nothing massaged.

### D. Contingency recovery — **pure upside**
App chargeback/deduction recovery: **20–25% of dollars we actually claw back**, $0 if nothing. No setup, no risk to them. Great door-opener ("let me find money the apps already owe you, free unless I get it back").

> **Vendor tools are billed to you at cost**, always. Our fee is the build + management, stated separately. We never take a cent from a vendor — so what we recommend is only ever what's right for you.

Setup fees stay well under the generic-agency $4k so the "launch" decision is easy.

## 5) Founding 5 — the pilot offer (exact terms)

- **Performance-first:** we put real money back **before** you pay a dollar. No savings, no fee.
- **Setup waived or deferred** until the savings show.
- **Founding monthly ~40–50% off, locked for life** (the table above).
- **In return:** a named case study with numbers, a video testimonial, and warm intros to 2–3 peer owners.
- **Strictly 5, then it closes.** After that, **regular is regular** — no more founding rate, ever. (This is what makes "locked for life" valuable and the urgency real.)

## 6) Worked examples (sanity check)

- **Imran (P1 takeout):** Commission Rescue $750 setup + **Starter $99/mo**. Moving even 30% of delivery off the apps saves ~$300–600/mo → **pays for itself in ~6 direct orders.** Founding 5: $0 until saving, then $79/mo for life.
- **Denise (P2 full-service):** Reviews + Win-back, **Growth $349/mo** + $1,500 setup. Each star ≈ 5–9% revenue; loyalty lifts repeat visits → setup back in weeks. Founding 5: $199/mo locked.
- **Priya (P3, 3 units):** Dashboard $2,000 setup + **$299 × 3 = $897/mo**, closing an 8–12% EBITDA gap across stores. Plus chargeback recovery at 20% of whatever we claw back. Founding 5: $179 × 3 = $537/mo locked.

## 7) What to put on the website now

Our own audit flagged that "no price anywhere" reads as "expensive," and the buyers all want a floor before they'll call. Recommendation:

- Add **"Plans from $99/mo — and you only pay once you're saving"** to the pricing section (a floor, not a full menu).
- Keep **setup quoted on the call** (it varies), but name the **paid audit option** ($300, credited) as the concrete low-risk entry for non-pilot skeptics.
- Keep the full menu off the public site — it's a sales-conversation tool (this doc).
