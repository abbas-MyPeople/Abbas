Hyphen Makeline

Automated under-counter makeline assembling bowls/salads.

$50k–100k

Visit Hyphen Makeline website →

What it costs

Capital purchase roughly $50,000-$100,000 per makeline. Hyphen says many customers reach payback in under a year through labor savings.

Best for

Fast-casual and ghost kitchens with bowl/salad/burrito formats doing high digital order volume.

Pros

  • Automates the lower assembly line while staff still work the top, cutting makeline labor sharply
  • High throughput (350+ meals/hour claimed) for digital and delivery orders
  • Backed by Chipotle and Cava investment, signaling real-world validation
  • Designed to slot in where a conventional makeline already sits

Cons

  • Large upfront capital cost, not a low-risk monthly subscription
  • Bowl/salad/assembly format only; not for plated or made-to-order complexity
  • Best ROI requires high digital order volume to justify

Integrations

Pulls digital orders from your website, app and third-party delivery; paired with Hyphen's CulinaryOS software.

Alternatives

Chef Robotics · Miso Robotics Flippy · PreciTaste

Our take: A strong bet for high-volume bowl concepts that can afford the capex, but the format limits and six-figure price tag put it out of reach for most single independents.
