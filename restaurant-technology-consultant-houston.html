What a restaurant technology consultant does

The modern restaurant runs on a dozen tools that rarely talk to each other: a POS and payment processor, one or more delivery apps, an online-ordering page, a phone that keeps ringing during the rush, reviews on three sites, a loyalty program you half-set-up, and whatever marketing you can find time for. Each one has a cost and a contract, and most owners never get a clear picture of where the money actually goes.

A restaurant technology consultant's job is to see that whole stack at once — then fix the highest-return problems first. That means starting from your numbers, not a product brochure: where are you paying 15–30% to delivery apps on orders you could own directly? How many calls go unanswered at 7pm? How many first-time guests never come back because nothing captured them? Good consulting is diagnosis before prescription. The tool comes last.
