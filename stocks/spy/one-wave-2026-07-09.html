Five numbers, repeated at every scale, tell you everything the market is doing. Here is today's reading.

A candle is where a price opened, the highest and lowest it reached, where it closed — and the shape those four numbers make: the body (open→close) and the wicks (the rejected extremes). That's the entire alphabet. Read it at the month, the week, the day, the hour, the quarter-hour — and the same sentences appear at every magnification, because each big candle is literally built out of the small ones. Every pattern named below was classified by measurement — body/range and wick/range ratios — not by opinion.

Marubozu body ≥85% of the range: one side in total control, no rejection.
Doji / spinning top body ≤12% / ≤40%: a stalemate; whoever pushed got pushed back.
Hammer / hanging man lower wick ≥2× body: a plunge that was bought back. At a low it's a floor forming; at a high, a warning.
Engulfing / star / soldiers multi-candle sentences: a reversal swallowing the prior bar, a gap-pause-reverse, three drives in a row.

Here is the technique's deepest point, visible in the numbers: June's monthly hanging-man wick — one line on the monthly chart — is the weekly hammer of June 12. That's why the levels agree across scales: they're not four analyses. They're one event, at four magnifications.

Method & honesty

Every read above was computed, not felt.

Classification rules: marubozu = body ≥85% of range; doji ≤12%; spinning top <40%; hammer/hanging man = lower wick ≥2× body with minimal upper wick; multi-candle patterns (engulfing, harami, stars, soldiers, crows, tweezers, inside/outside bars) by strict body/extreme comparisons; swing structure by fractal pivots; horizontal zones by pivot clustering; diagonals by two-pivot lines with values computed per session. The engine and this page's generator are committed alongside the data.

Data bases, stated plainly: monthly/weekly wicks = daily-close extremes (FRED official series; intraday extremes unavailable — so a monthly wick here understates the true wick). Daily bodies real for all 16; daily wicks real only Jul 7–9 (recorded live by this repo's snapshot pipeline, SPY×10.04). Hourly/15-minute candles built from recorded 5-minute closes — intra-bar extremes slightly understated; Jul 8's first ~65 minutes weren't captured. Today's close (7,543.64) is the official print; today's intraday shape is recorded through 1:46 PM. One-minute data: never obtained, therefore never shown. Where the instrument couldn't reach, the page says so instead of imagining — that rule is the technique.
