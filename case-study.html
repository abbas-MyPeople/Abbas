Cost: orders lost in the busiest hours, invisibly

Three tablets, all beeping, all night.

DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub each on their own tablet. Staff accepted each order, then re-typed it into the POS by hand. In a rush, orders came out late, wrong — or vanished.

Cost: lost & wrong orders · staff hours every shift

Invisible to the newest front door.

Ask ChatGPT "best halal food near Spring, TX" and we weren't in the answer — our website literally blocked AI crawlers (an HTTP 403), so no assistant could read our menu, hours, or address. Worse: AI thought we were two different restaurants.

Cost: absent from where discovery is moving fastest

The month-end commission bill.

The delivery apps took ~30% of every order they touched — and kept the customer's name, number, and habits for themselves. Direct orders ran just ~8.8% of our mix while 20%+ sat on the 30%-fee apps.

Cost: thousands per year, plus the customer relationship

Built · found in our own sales data

Our data found money we didn't know we had.

We analyzed ~1,500 of our own POS orders and found that guests who order from both cuisines — Chinese and Indian/Pakistani — spend a 63% higher ticket. Nothing was steering anyone there. Now the menu, the website, and the phone agent all nudge toward the pairing.

That's what "analytics" means here: not a dashboard for its own sake, but your own numbers finding your next dollar.

An AI employee on the phone — live, right now.

Nothing we could buy would say our dish names right, speak our guests' languages, or write a clean order into Clover. What does "the site was 403 to bots" mean, and why does it matter?

The old site returned an HTTP 403 error to crawlers, so ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity and Claude couldn't read the menu, hours or address — invisible in the fastest-growing discovery channel. The rebuilt, code-built site is now fully machine-readable, with full schema and a statically-rendered menu.

Why does ordering direct instead of through the apps matter so much?

The apps take ~30% per order plus fees, and they own the customer. Direct ordering costs a fraction. At our place, direct runs ~8.8% of the mix against 20%+ on the 30%-fee apps — move even part of that to direct and the difference drops straight to the bottom line.
